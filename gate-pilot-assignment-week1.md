# Week 1 — Your To-Do

Two of you, one tiny game, two AI agents: **Builder A drives Codex, Builder B drives Claude Code.** You build the same little Unity game — one-screen top-down survivor, plain shapes, no art — by directing the AI one step at a time.

The game isn't the point. **You are.** The AI writes the code; it can't play the game. You can. Your job is to play each step and feel when it's wrong — too floaty, fire rate too fast, enemy jitters — and push the AI until it's solid. Every time the AI hands you something that *looks* fine but *feels* wrong when you play it, that's the thing we're hunting. Write those down.

All the details you need — the shared starter project, the six prompts to paste, what "solid" means for each step, the attestation form — are in **`gate-pilot-week1.md`**. This page is just the to-do.

## Do this

1. **Grab the shared starter project** (in the kit: Starter project skeleton). Plain shapes, tags set, no scripts. Both of you start from the exact same one. **Don't download a reference game** — if you copy working code, there's nothing for you to feel, and feeling it is the whole job.

2. **Build the game, six steps, in order:** move → aim → shoot → enemy → kill+respawn → score. For each step: paste the step's prompt (same words for both of you), run it, *play it*, tell the AI what's off, play again, until it's solid. Only then move to the next step. When you land a good number (move speed, fire rate, chase speed), lock it with your partner so you're both judging "solid" the same way.

3. **Make one skill you actually verify.** Somewhere in there the AI will produce something worth keeping. Save the best recipe, get it solid and reusable, and fill in the attestation (kit → Attestation) — including the "Did not test" list, honestly. A recipe that ran once doesn't count; one you played, tried to break, and it held does.

4. **Run the earn-silent trial** (kit → P8) the moment that skill is verified. Reuse it on the next step *without playtesting first* — trust it, just run it. Then play it. Did it hold, or did skipping the play-test let something slip? Either answer is gold.

## Hand back

- The working game.
- The one verified skill + its attestation.
- Your notes: every "it looked fine but played wrong" moment.
- A list of anything you had to do by hand because no tool did it — this is what tells me what to build next, so don't skip it.
- The earn-silent result: held or broke.

## The one rule

Be honest about what's shaky. The best thing you can hand me isn't a finished game — it's "the AI wrote this, it compiled clean, and it felt wrong the second I played it." That gap, between *compiles* and *feels right*, is everything.
